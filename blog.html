POETRY 2026.03.13

The End Poem

In the void where endstone floats,

Between the chorus flowers' notes,

A builder stands with empty hands,

And dreams of far and distant lands.


The dragon circles overhead,

Through purple mist and starlight spread,

Each wing-beat writes upon the air,

A testament — that we were there.

LITERATURE 2026.03.13

On Building in Public

There is a particular kind of courage required to build something when nobody is watching. It's not the courage of the battlefield or the stage — it's quieter. It's the courage of the first commit to an empty repository, the first line of code written at 2 AM with no audience, no applause, no validation except the blinking cursor that says: "Continue."

The best things are built in silence. The End dimension teaches this — you arrive alone, you face the dragon alone, and the credits roll whether anyone sees them or not. The building is the point. The rest is echo.
